What we leave out

Ingredients you will never find in our formulas.

Our formulation brief starts with a 'never list'. If an ingredient has a poor safety profile, limited evidence, or serves only a marketing function — it doesn't make the cut.

  • ×

    Synthetic Fragrance (Parfum)

    A documented sensitiser linked to contact dermatitis. Serves no functional benefit to the skin. Banned across our entire range.

  • ×

    Essential Oils

    Despite a natural origin, many essential oils are potent irritants with high sensitisation potential. We exclude all of them.

  • ×

    Parabens

    Replaced with equally effective, better-tolerated preservation systems given growing consumer concern.

  • ×

    Sulphates (SLS/SLES)

    High-foaming sulphates disrupt the skin's natural pH and lipid balance, contributing to barrier compromise.

  • ×

    Silicone Fillers

    Used in mass-market formulas to create temporary smoothness. They don't treat skin — they mask it.

  • ×

    Mineral Oil

    A petroleum-derived occlusive without meaningful benefit. Replaced by Squalane and plant-derived alternatives.

How we formulate

A rigorous process before anything reaches your skin.

Our formulation process is built around a single question: does this actually work, and is it safe? Every step exists to answer that honestly.

1

Evidence review

Every candidate ingredient is reviewed against published clinical literature. We look at study design, concentration used, and outcome measures — not marketing summaries.

2

Concentration benchmarking

We identify the minimum effective dose from the clinical literature and formulate at or above that level. No token amounts included for label value.

3

Compatibility testing

Active ingredients are tested in combination to verify stability, pH compatibility, and absence of antagonistic interactions across the full shelf life.

4

Dermatologist review

Each formula is reviewed by a board-certified dermatologist before clinical testing. Safety is assessed for sensitive, reactive, and compromised skin types.

5

Clinical efficacy study

We conduct 8–12 week clinical studies on diverse skin type cohorts, measuring outcomes against placebo controls using standardised dermatological methods.

6

Stability and shelf-life validation

Accelerated stability testing at multiple temperature conditions. No formula is released with a shelf life shorter than 18 months post-opening.

Barrier health

Why your skin barrier is the foundation of everything.

The skin barrier is a thin but critical structure that regulates moisture, blocks environmental irritants, and prevents pathogens from entering. When it's functioning well, your skin can tolerate active ingredients and retain hydration.

When it's compromised — through over-exfoliation, harsh cleansers, or environmental stress — redness, breakouts, dehydration, and sensitivity follow. Most skin concerns have a barrier component.

Moisture retentionA healthy barrier prevents transepidermal water loss, keeping skin plump and resilient.
Irritant protectionA functioning barrier blocks environmental pollutants, allergens, and bacteria from penetrating the dermis.
Active toleranceBarrier-compromised skin reacts to ingredients that healthy skin tolerates easily. Repair first — then introduce actives.

Questions

Ingredient FAQ

  • Can I use multiple actives in the same routine?

    Yes — most of our serums are compatible and work well together. The main rule: keep the Retinal Bio-Lift Night Serum to the PM and don't layer it with the Glow + Marks Serum's Vitamin C in the same session. Apply from lightest to richest texture, and finish with the Barrier Cream.

  • Why does concentration matter?

    The clinical evidence for an ingredient is always at a specific concentration. Below that threshold, the ingredient may do very little. We formulate at or above the minimum effective dose established in peer-reviewed studies.

  • Are your formulas suitable for sensitive skin?

    Most are, yes. Every formula is tested on sensitive skin types. Our Peptide Repair Barrier Serum and Exosome PDRN Repair Serum are specifically designed for reactive, stressed-looking skin. Sensitive skin types should introduce the Retinal Bio-Lift Night Serum slowly — a couple of nights a week to start.

  • Are all formulas fragrance-free?

    Yes — 100% fragrance-free, including essential oils and parfum derivatives. Fragrance is the leading cause of cosmetic sensitisation. It is banned across our entire range.
