Questions

FAQ

How accurate is the serum recommendation?

The quiz matches your answers against our formulation matrix — mapping skin goal, type, texture preference, timing, and experience level to the serum most likely to deliver results. We also apply safety overrides: beginners are guided away from harsh actives, and morning users are matched to photostable formulas. While no quiz replaces a skin consultation, the recommendation will get you started on the right formula for your primary concern.

Can I use the primary and secondary serum together?

In most cases, yes. Check the AM/PM timing labels — if both are marked 'AM + PM', you can layer them morning and evening (lightest texture first). The main exception is mixing exfoliating acids (AHAs/BHAs) with retinol — these should be used on alternate evenings to avoid sensitivity. Our routine guides provide specific layering instructions for each combination.

What if I have multiple skin concerns?

Choose your single highest-priority concern — the one that bothers you most day-to-day. The quiz is designed to find your best starting formula, and the secondary recommendation adds complementary coverage. Once your skin has adjusted (usually 4–6 weeks), you can introduce additional serums to address secondary concerns.

Are all serums fragrance-free?

Yes. Every Sebble Skin serum is 100% fragrance-free, including essential oils and parfum derivatives. All formulas are tested under dermatologist supervision and are suitable for fragrance-sensitive skin. If you have a diagnosed contact allergy to a specific ingredient, always check the full INCI list on the product page before use.

What does the Barrier Cream do in the routine?

The Sebble Barrier Cream is your routine's finishing step — it seals in the active ingredients your serum has delivered and prevents trans-epidermal water loss. Formulated with Ceramides and Shea Butter, it strengthens the outer skin barrier overnight. Apply a pea-sized amount after your serum has fully absorbed, morning or evening.
