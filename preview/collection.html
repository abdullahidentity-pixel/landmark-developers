How to choose

Choosing the right serum for your skin

01

Identify your primary concern

Start with one concern — hydration, brightness, texture, or sensitivity. Layering too many actives at once reduces effectiveness.

02

Check for active conflicts

AHAs and retinol should be used on separate evenings. Vitamin C pairs well with SPF but avoid high-dose niacinamide at the same time.

03

Start slow, build gradually

Introduce new actives every 2 weeks. Begin with every-other-night use and build up as your skin adjusts. Always patch test first.

04

Layer in the right order

Apply thinnest to thickest — water-based serums first, then oil-based, then moisturiser. Allow 60 seconds between each layer.

Questions

FAQ

Can I use more than one serum at the same time?

Yes — most Sebble serums are designed to layer. Apply in order of lightest to heaviest texture, and allow 60 seconds between each for absorption. Avoid layering AHAs with retinol on the same evening.

How do I know which serum is right for my skin?

Start with your primary concern — whether that's hydration, texture, sensitivity, or glow. Use our filter above to narrow down by concern, or take our Skin Quiz for a personalised recommendation.

Are these serums suitable for sensitive skin?

All Sebble formulas are fragrance-free and developed with sensitive skin in mind. For highly reactive skin, we recommend the Peptide Repair Serum as your first step. Always patch test before full facial use.

Do I need to use SPF with these serums?

Yes. Any formula containing Vitamin C, AHAs, or retinol increases photosensitivity. We recommend a broad-spectrum SPF 30+ every morning, regardless of which serum you use.

Are your products vegan and cruelty-free?

Yes. All Sebble products are 100% vegan and certified cruelty-free. We do not test on animals at any stage of production, and we do not sell in markets that require animal testing.
